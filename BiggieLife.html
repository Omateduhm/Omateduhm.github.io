Biggie's Legacy

Biggie was one of, if not the, most famous and popular rappers of his generation. He found was able to find his place in the recording rooms rather then on the street. As for the popularity of his music it can be traced to how he relates the things that happened in his life in his music. He gave people the chance to listen to music they themselves could relate to.

Album Success

Two of Biggie's albums went Platinum during his short spanned, but deeply impactful, carrer as a rapper. What follows is a list of those platinum albums:

  • Ready to Die
  • Life After Death

After his death his legacy continued with the release of multiple posthumous albums:

  • Born Again
  • Duets: The Final Chapter
  • The King & I

Out of these three posthumous albums two of them went platinum, those being:

  • Born Again
  • Duets: The Final Chapter

Biggie's rapping career began in 1993, and his first album release came in 1994 with the second coming in 1997 sixteen days after his passing which was the original planned release date. In a short span of four years Biggie, aka Christopher George Latore Wallace, was able to have two of his own completed albums go platinum as well as have two more do the same years after his death. If this isn't enough to cement him in history as a rapper, then nothing can.
